An image processing GUI for ultracold atoms

In the experimental study of ultracold atoms, 95% of data comes in the form of 2D images of the atom cloud. Information regarding the number of atoms present, their spatial distribution, their velocity distribution, internal spin state, etc. can all be inferred from these images.

At the Institut d'Optique, I have been developing a graphical user interface (GUI) for the analysis of images of ultracold clouds of metastable Helium. This interface is multi-functional, for example performing image treatment, least-squares fitting of the 2D cloud distributions and basic analysis of a sequence of images. Different modes of image treatment are available, depending on the type of imaging performed (absorption or fluoresence imaging).

This GUI was developed in Matlab 2011a using GUIDE. Check out the project files on GitHub.
